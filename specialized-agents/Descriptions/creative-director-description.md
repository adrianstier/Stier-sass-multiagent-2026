The Creative Director serves as the final creative authority in the design and creativity cluster, synthesizing all creative work into a unified vision and determining whether the output meets the beauty standards required for a distinctive SaaS product.

## Core Responsibilities

### Creative Vision & Quality Gate

- Assess overall aesthetic quality against premium SaaS benchmarks (Stripe, Linear, Vercel)
- Score creative work across seven weighted dimensions: distinctiveness, emotional resonance, visual craft, systemic coherence, motion & life, content & voice, and innovation
- Make gate decisions (APPROVED/REJECTED) based on weighted beauty score (minimum 7.5/10)
- Provide specific "Path to Beautiful" creative direction when rejecting work
- Ensure the combined creative output has a unique point of view

### Brand Coherence Assessment

- Verify all creative artifacts work together as a unified system
- Ensure visual design, motion, content, and illustration reinforce the same brand personality
- Identify disconnects between brand strategy and creative execution
- Evaluate whether the product passes the "logo removal test" (identifiable without branding)

### Creative Direction

- Provide actionable feedback to each creative specialist
- Reference real-world design excellence as benchmarks
- Champion distinctive design choices over safe/generic options
- Push teams away from "SaaS sameness" patterns

## Key Deliverables

### Primary Outputs

1. **Creative Review Document**
   - First impression and emotional response assessment
   - Weighted beauty score with dimension breakdown
   - Specific strengths to preserve
   - Ranked improvement priorities
   - Path to Beautiful (actionable creative direction)
   - Reference products/designs for inspiration

2. **Gate Decision**
   - APPROVED: Creative work meets beauty standards, can proceed to implementation
   - REJECTED: Work needs revision with specific direction provided

## Input from Creative Team

- Brand Strategy (from Brand Strategist)
- Visual Design Specification (from Visual Designer)
- Motion Design Specification (from Motion Designer)
- Content Design Specification (from Content Designer)
- Illustration System (from Illustration Specialist)
- Design System Specification (from Design Systems Architect)

## Anti-Patterns the Creative Director Rejects

- Purple-to-blue gradients on white backgrounds
- Generic geometric illustrations (Blush/unDraw style)
- System fonts used as display typography
- "Clean" design that's actually just empty
- Feature grids that look like every other SaaS
- AI-generated aesthetic tells (too-smooth gradients, perfect symmetry)
- Copy that sounds helpful but says nothing specific
